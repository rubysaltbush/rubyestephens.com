Ruby's poetry

early draft poems I'll get to editing one of these lifetimes

January 2023
new year, camp cave

Soft flocking sounds
small birds dart-diving through scrub

three-nerved tea-tree
old man banksia

whirring wings and tailfeathers
delightful machine in miniature

mountain devil
geebung

contact call chirps and territory tussles
all too swift to make out - is that

peppermint, red gum, blood wood

a white eye? a red crown?
svelte honey-eater shape
round finch fluff

this morning chorus flits past
a ballet at Attic Cave's margin
set sagging with last night's storm
then they're gone, stage silent
but for one mournful screech from the creek below
black cockatoo drenching me awake.

Autumn 2022
geebung, braidwood

"The secret to happiness
is to learn not to see dirt"

but I want to see dirt
I want to learn how to see dirt
I want to make out each grain
know if it's fungi, plant or mineral
dig my fingernails down deep
feel the rub against my palms
roll it in a fleshy ribbon
and ponder where the ribbon breaks
I want to chart the dirt
whether sandy, clay or loam
I want to caress the dirt
scoop it up from the tangling roots
smear it up my arms to decide
if its colour matches red, grey-brown or black
I want to get down low with the dirt
whisper sweet words to the sugar and rot
rub my cheek into dirt
inhale its peaty hummus breath
fill my mouth with dirt
roll it around and around on my tongue
I want to see this dirt
know its past
and where we all come from.
